COLLECTION OF
BARON MÓR LIPÓT HERZOG (1869-1934)

A Jewish Art Collector in the Pre-War Hungary

Baron Herzog Mór Lipót's collection, at its zenith, comprised about 2500 artworks, displayed in the Andrássy Avenue palace of the Andrássy family. The private collection, showcasing world-renowned masters, included masterpieces by El Greco, Lucas Cranach, Corot, and Renoir, among others. Herzog Mór Lipót began dealing with artworks under the influence of another famous art collector-businessman of the era, Marcell Nemes (1866–1930). The wealth of the Herzog family, largely derived from tobacco production, allowed them to acquire not only popular works but also unconventional pieces on the international art market. Herzog Mór Lipót later purchased valuable pieces from Nemes's collection, including the now globally famous paintings by El Greco.

The reputation and recognition of the collection gradually grew in the 1920s, leading to the creation of an illustrated catalogue featuring the finest pieces in 1927. The expansion of the collection came to a halt with the death of Herzog Mór Lipót in 1934. The artworks were inherited by his wife, Hatvany-Deutsch Janka, and their three children: Herzog Erzsébet, Herzog András, and Herzog István. By the end of World War II, the collection had been dispersed. Some parts still adorn Hungarian museums and other public institutions. Another group of artworks was taken to the Soviet Union at that time, and some pieces have yet to resurface. Remaining artworks are found in Germany, Poland, and lost works occasionally reappear in international and domestic auctions. The heirs of Herzog tried for many years to recover art looted from their antecendent. In 2010, his heirs sued the Hungarian government for the return of more than 40 paintings seized during World War II, valued in excess of US$ 100 million.

Although the collection contained several handicrafts and furnitures, this website shows a selection of the paintings. Recommended literature for further information about the collection: Elveszett örökség, ed. Péter Molnos, Kieselbach Galéria, Budapest, 2017, ISBN: 987-615-5199-13-4.
